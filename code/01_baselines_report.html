2. Accuracy vs. F1 on challenging slices — the headline finding

This is the key evidence for the project's thesis. On hard slices, accuracy looks fine (drops only ~0.5 pp, sometimes even rises) — but F1 drops sharply on the mixed-sentiment slice (≈10–11 pp). This is exactly the "accuracy is misleading" phenomenon: the overall number stays high because most hard reviews happen to be predicted positive, but the model's ability to correctly recall positives vs. negatives collapses.

4. Takeaways for the presentation

  • Best baseline achieves 88.29% accuracy on the full IMDb test set.
  • Accuracy alone is misleading. On the mixed-sentiment slice, accuracy stays around 88-89% — but F1 drops by ≈10-11 percentage points, from ~88.19% down to ~76.75%.
  • On negation-heavy reviews, F1 drops ~3-4 pp — models literally get tripped up by phrases like "not bad".
  • Hybrid features (TF-IDF + lexicon + negation) give the best overall numbers, but do not close the gap on hard slices, which motivates moving to context-aware models (CNN, BERT, LLMs).
  • Headline sentence for the talk: "Our best model hits 88% accuracy, but on mixed-sentiment reviews its F1 collapses from 88% to 77%. That is exactly why accuracy alone is not enough."
